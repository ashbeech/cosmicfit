## 3. The Textures

_Learn to identify the textiles that serve your best shapes by touch alone._

Build your mobile layers with materials that feel alive. Run the glide test on every garment before you buy it. Place one palm flat under the fabric and press your other hand on top. Shift your palms in opposite directions. The cloth must slip and fold without fighting your grip. Seek out Washed silk for slips and blouses softened until the sheen turns to candlelight. Choose Cloud-soft lambswool for jumpers and cardigans that shelter without weight. Rely on Heavy silk crepe for dresses that trace the body with fluid, hidden depth. Finally, use Boiled wool for unstructured protective coats that stay soft at every edge. These materials maintain your clean geometry and keep your overall temperature cool. ## 5. The Hardware

_Evaluate your hardware by weight and shine to ground your wardrobe in physical reality._

Plating lies to the eye, but gravity tells the truth. Conduct the drop test on any chain or cuff. Rest the piece in your palm and assess its sheer density. If an item lacks the hefty mass you expect from its scale, put it back. True weight is required to ground your clean geometry. Keep your temperature strictly cool. Anchor your look with matt silver, gunmetal, or pewter. Avoid anything designed to look old. Artificially weathered bronze, beaten copper, and patinated surfaces create unnecessary noise in a crisp, modern wardrobe. Maintain a strict, polished surface, and leave high-shine polished chrome out of the equation entirely.
