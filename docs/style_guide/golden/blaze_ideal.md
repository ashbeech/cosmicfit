# Your Cosmic Style Guide

### Blaze

---

## 1. The Blueprint

You dress to occupy space and project high visual volume. Before a garment registers on your body, you evaluate how much space it commands and how generously it moves. You seek pieces that amplify your natural scale.

Your wardrobe is built on an expansive, theatrical rhythm. The core formula for your style is expansive colour + athletic space + one theatrical finish. Picture a voluminous saffron yellow parachute silk trench coat thrown over wide leg ivory cotton twill trousers, anchored entirely by a pair of massive polished gold hoop earrings.

You build your collection rapidly and enthusiastically. A functional wardrobe for you acts as a stage, demanding pieces that are ready to perform the moment you buy them. You do not wait for the perfect occasion to wear your best items.

Rely on the radius test for every new piece. Put the garment on and stretch your arms out wide. If you feel physically restricted or visually shrunk, take it off immediately. Trust the pieces that allow you to take up maximum space.

---

## 2. The Palette

Your colour world operates on a strictly hot warm temperature. You thrive in sunlit, radiant zones that feel generous and alive.

Build your foundation with warm, expansive neutrals that serve specific structural roles. Deep bronze and golden camel anchor your heavy coats and trousers. Warm ivory acts as your primary base canvas. Rich terracotta and warm sand bridge your layers. These shades provide the sunlit baseline you require.

Your accent rule demands deliberate scale. Introduce one bold, radiant tone per outfit to capture the eye. A scarf in vibrant vermilion or a bag in bright sunflower yellow provides all the theatrical heat required.

When a look feels too visually overwhelming, rely on a soft washed peach. This is your relief colour. Use it as a lightweight knit layer under a heavy coat to give the outfit breathing room without dropping the temperature.

Pass over stark optic white, icy greys, and muddy pastels. These colours are too cold and will extinguish your required visual impact.

Your biggest trap is wearing a restrictive dark monochrome look that stifles your presence. If you find yourself in an uninterrupted navy or black outfit, break the silhouette with a massive polished gold cuff or a bright poppy red shoe to pull the focus back and restore your theatrical scale.

---

## 3. The Textures

Fabric is your amplifier. You evaluate materials by how brilliantly they catch the light and how much physical space they allow you to occupy.

### The Good

Your best materials rank strictly by their capacity to provide athletic mobility and theatrical polish:

**Parachute silk**: Essential for voluminous outerwear that catches the wind and moves dynamically around your body.

**Crisp cotton twill**: The ultimate choice for wide leg trousers that offer architectural space without feeling heavy.

**High shine taffeta**: Brilliant for evening pieces that need to bounce light and create an unmistakable silhouette.

**Technical mesh**: Ideal for athletic outer layers that hold an architectural shape while breathing easily.

**Lightweight linen**: Brilliant for wide summer trousers that stay airy without collapsing your volume.

**Chunky merino wool**: Your foundation for oversized knits that offer generous warmth and massive scale.

**Polished calfskin**: Perfect for footwear and accessories to add a slick, radiant surface to your layers.

### The Bad

Stiff, scratchy, and physically confining fabrics will immediately make you feel trapped. Rigid raw denim, heavy tweed, and unyielding synthetic plastics force your body into a box and completely kill your athletic mobility. If a material fights your stride, it is entirely wrong for your expansive style.

### The Sweet Spot

Your ideal pairing happens when a highly polished surface meets a matt athletic fabric. A crisp cotton twill jumpsuit worn with a high shine calfskin boot creates the exact performative tension you need.

Rely on the bounce test in the fitting room. Walk briskly in the garment and watch how the fabric responds. If it moves generously and catches the light, it belongs in your wardrobe. If it stays rigid and flat, walk away.

---

## 4. The Occasions

Your core formula of expansive colour + athletic space + one theatrical finish translates powerfully into any environment.

### At Work

Professional settings require you to maintain your radiant scale without losing mobility. Choose wide leg fluid trousers in golden camel paired with an ivory silk blouse that offers generous movement. Add a single oversized polished brass watch. The tailoring is expansive, keeping your physical space large and unmistakable.

### Intimate Energy

For evening settings, lean fully into the theatrical finish. A voluminous high shine taffeta dress in vibrant vermilion creates an immediate sunlit impact. The fabrics should reflect the room lighting and invite a celebratory mood.

### Daily Movement

Weekends and errands require the exact same philosophy of generous scale. Even for a trip to the post office, you need clothes that broadcast intent, because paying a utility bill does not require collapsing your silhouette. Choose a bright terracotta oversized knit and wide leg athletic cotton trousers.

The formula holds across all contexts; only the volume of the finish changes.

---

## 5. The Hardware

Hardware acts as the crucial theatrical finish in your generous outfits. It provides the necessary mirror polish to bounce light back into the room.

### The Metals

Your metal strategy thrives on a mixed free approach with a strict demand for statement scale. Seek out massive polished brass, bright yellow gold, and gleaming copper. These finishes look as though they have been dipped in sunlight. They bring a joyful, highly polished weight to your athletic silhouettes.

Completely exclude blackened, heavily oxidised, or softly brushed matt metals. They are too quiet and subdued for your radiant style.

### The Stones

Look for stones that project massive warmth and bold colour. Citrine, fire opal, bright coral, and yellow diamonds possess the fiery energy you require. Avoid cloudy moonstones or delicate seed pearls that disappear entirely against your bold clothes.

### Tip

Always apply the pick it up weight test. If a brass buckle, a gold cuff, or a bag chain feels hollow or light in your hand, it fails immediately. Your hardware must possess genuine physical density to anchor your expansive fabrics.

---

## 6. The Code

A private set of rules to keep your wardrobe feeling generous and aligned.

### Lean Into

Building outfits around the core formula of expansive colour + athletic space + one theatrical finish.

Oversized generous silhouettes that allow you to move with total athletic freedom.

Highly polished metals that bring a radiant mirror shine to your look.

Warm sunlit neutrals punctuated by bold theatrical brights.

### Avoid

Constricting tight tailoring that forces you to take small careful steps.

Muted dusty palettes that dilute your required visual volume.

Delicate invisible jewellery that fails to make a statement.

### Consider

Evaluating the physical scale of a piece by asking if the garment allows you to move with maximum extension and readiness.

Prioritising cost per wear. A beautifully made voluminous silk coat is a true investment compared to a cheap replacement that loses its radiant finish after one season.

Checking for five to ten year longevity. Ask yourself if you would still wear this bold piece in five to ten years before bringing it into your wardrobe.

---

## 7. The Accessory

Accessories represent the final phase of your formula, supplying the one theatrical finish required to balance your expansive colour + athletic space. You do not need a clutter of small items. The principle is strict: stick to one or two strong pieces per look.

Statement eyewear is essential for your performative aesthetic. Choose oversized acetate frames in tortoiseshell or bright warm tones that act as a theatrical mask.

Substantial belts serve as your ultimate architectural tool. A wide thick leather belt with a massive polished brass buckle provides immediate structure over a voluminous dress.

Footwear must offer athletic mobility with a polished edge. A chunky platform boot or a bold thick soled leather trainer in a bright tone grounds your generous layers perfectly.

Use the stage test before leaving the house. Put on your chosen accessory and step back. If it feels like the focal point of a stage, you are ready. If it fades into the background, swap it for something with greater visual weight.

---

## 8. The Pattern

Your approach to pattern is joyful, large scale, and highly legible. You want patterns that broadcast unmistakable intent from across the street.

For tailored pieces like heavy coats or wide trousers, rely on oversized plaids and massive block stripes in your warm colour palette. The pattern must feel generous and bold, perfectly matching your physical scale.

For fluid pieces like silk shirts, dresses, and scarves, look for large scale tropical motifs, giant abstract brushstrokes, and vivid animal prints. The scale of the pattern must be huge and unmistakable.

Pass over tiny ditsy florals, microscopic dots, and faint faded tartans. These patterns are far too timid and cluttered for your expansive style.

Rely on the twenty foot rule. Look at the pattern from across the room. If the print remains bold and entirely legible from twenty feet away, it works. If it blurs into a muddy texture, it lacks the theatrical energy you require and should be left behind.
